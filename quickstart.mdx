## Prerequisites

Before you begin, ensure you have the following:

<Callout kind="info">

- A modern web browser like Chrome, Firefox, or Safari
- An email address for account verification
- Optional: A GitHub account for advanced integrations

</Callout>

Silly Smile works entirely in your browser, so no downloads or installations are required.

## Create Your Account

Sign up for Silly Smile in under a minute.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit the Dashboard" icon="monitor">
    Navigate to [https://dashboard.example.com](https://dashboard.example.com).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register" icon="user-plus">
    Click **Sign Up** and enter your email and password.
    
    Verify your email via the confirmation link sent to your inbox.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log In" icon="log-in">
    Return to the dashboard and sign in with your credentials.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create Your First Documentation Space

Organize your project docs by creating a dedicated space.

<Steps>
  <Step title="New Space" icon="plus">
    Click **New Space** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Space" icon="settings">
    Enter a name like `My Project Docs` and select a visibility: public, private, or workspace-only.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save" icon="save">
    Click **Create**. Your space appears in the sidebar.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Add Initial Content

Populate your space with your first document. Choose your preferred method:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Markdown Editor" icon="edit-3">
    
    Open the editor and paste Markdown content.
    
````markdown
# Welcome to My Docs

This is your first page.

## Features

- Easy editing
- Live preview
````
    
    Preview updates in real-time as you type.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Upload File" icon="upload">
    
    Drag and drop a `.md` or `.mdx` file into the upload area.
    
    <Callout kind="tip">
      Supports up to 10MB per file. Larger docs require Git sync.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Import from GitHub" icon="github">
    
    Connect your repo and import docs automatically.
    
    Use this command in your terminal:
    
````bash
git clone https://github.com/yourusername/silly-smile-docs.git
````
    
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
